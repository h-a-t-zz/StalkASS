ANEURYSM & AVM MIRACLE 5K
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
The Delta Kappa chapter of Alpha Phi Omega will be hosting the Aneurysm and AVM Miracle 5K on Saturday, April 18th in honor of Gilad Nissim, a close family friend of a Delta Kappa brother, Hilary. Hilary met Gilad Nissim (1993-2013) when she was two years old. He was her brother’s best friend, and became an older brother figure to her. When Gilad was a freshman at Towson University, he was on his way to class when a blood vessel in his brain burst due to AVM. He remained in a coma for 8 months, until he passed away on November 17th 2013 at the age of 19. Gilad was incredibly friendly and outgoing, making himself known wherever he goes. Hilary organized this race in order to keep Gilad’s spirit alive. The 5K will begin at 9:00 am. It will start at the WrecRoom in Woodruff Residential Center (1495 Clifton Road), wind through Lullwater Park, and end in front of Woodruff. We will be giving prizes to the top three male and female finishers. Enjoy the spring weather by going for a run with your friends, and benefit a charity while you are at it! Please wear red or burgundy, the color associated with Aneurysm and AVM awareness, as well as Gilad's favorite color (because of Iron Man). All proceeds will be given to The Aneurysm and AVM Foundation (TAAF). TAAF is a nonprofit organization which funds research for and raises awareness of aneurysms and other vascular malformations, as well as supports the patients and families of patients with these conditions. For more information visit http:// www.taafonline.org/ For any questions or concerns, contact Hilary Druckman at hilary.shelli.druckman@emory.edu We hope to see you there!
