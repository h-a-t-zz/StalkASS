Bone Marrow Typing Drive @ Quora in Downtown Mountain View
Dustin went
Join us at Quora's new headquarters in downtown Mountain View on Friday 6/14 from 11am to 2pm. Open to the public. East and South Asians are underrepresented in the national bone marrow registry. Help Nina, William, Rajesh and others you may know by spreading the word about registering, especially to folks who work near downtown Mountain View. 10 minutes and a cheek swab is all it takes. See you Friday! http:// savethreelives.weebly .com/ Nina, William and Rajesh need your help and are in urgent need of a matching marrow donor. NINA POLVANICH LOUIE - savenina.com Nina was diagnosed with diffuse large B-cell lymphoma in September of 2012. She endured 7 rounds of aggressive chemotherapy at UCLA to treat the disease, but after only two months of remission, the lymphoma recurred in her brain. A Stanford and Harvard alum and a Texas native, Nina is the loving mother of a two year-old son and has less than 2 months to find a bone marrow donor. WILLIAM TSE William is the brother of Marvin Tse, a Googler. William, 24 years old, went to college at USC and received an offer from KPMG to start in the fall. Unfortunately he was diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (AML) in January 2013. He is in urgent need of a marrow transplant. This procedure is William’s only hope for his long-term survival. RAJESH SHARMA Rajesh has an aggressive form of lymphoma that can no longer be treated with chemotherapy. He's a member of the legal team at Genentech, and they've reached out to us for help. Rajesh is a wonderful husband and a doting father to his 8 year old daughter. His best chance of cure is finding a match ideally from someone of South Asian descent. This procedure is Rajesh's last chance for survival. Are you East Asian or South Asian? If you are East or South Asian, please strongly consider joining the registry. Nina (Chinese-Thai), William (Chinese) and Rajesh (Indian) are minorities and there is a severe shortage of minority donors in the national registry. Patients will mostly match with a donor within the same ethnic group. What is involved in registering? It takes 10 minutes to register and the entire process is painless. A sample of your tissue type is collected by a swab of cheek cells. Your tissue sample will be sent to Be The Match registry and use the results to match you to patients. In addition, you would need to: - Be between the ages of 18-44 (Patients have much higher survival rate from donors in this age group) - Fill out a consent form - Provide a cheek swab - Commit to donate to any patient What is the donation process? Does it hurt? In the past, Bone Marrow Extraction was the sole technique available and required a needle to extract bone marrow from inside of the pelvic bone. The procedure was done under general or local anesthesia so the donor felt no pain, though it was possible to have slight lower back soreness for a couple days. Today, the majority of donations today do not require surgery. In the PBSC (peripheral blood stem cell) procedure, your blood is filtered to extract bone marrow stem cells. The experience is similar to donating blood. Read more at http://www.aadp.org/ donation-process/ How long does it take to donate marrow? The Peripheral Blood Stem Cell process takes 6-8 hours over 1-2 days. Most donors report the worst part is boredom. :) Don’t I need my bone marrow? You sure do. But only 5-10% of a donor’s bone marrow is donated, and your body naturally replenishes its supply within a couple weeks.
