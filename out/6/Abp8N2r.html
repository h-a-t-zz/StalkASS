Organic Math: Cycles Part 1 [Online Event, Time in PST]
Dustin went
These discussions are structured in a way to be enjoyed and understood irrelevant of past experience or enjoyment of math! IF YOU THINK YOU HATE MATH YOU SHOULD JOIN!!! Hello! "If a full moon falls on Sunday, on what day of the week will the next full moon fall?" Sounds boring? You'd be surprised at what one uncovers... (check out how an edited version of one of our previous discussions (for which we assumed prior knowledge with algebra) - http:// organicmath.org/ 556444-transcript-2.p hp) I've been doing these math meeting/classes for a LONG time, going through various iterations. Now we're taking them online. If you're even remotely interested, you should come since you don't have to go anywhere and it's just 2 hours. You have 2 hours, right? We are trying to recreate what we see as a true mathematical process -- one in which we don't know the answers, or even if there are answers. We'll be stumbling around, not sure what to do. That's what mathematics is really like. At any point in the discussion if you have questions please feel free to ask (definitely including "I'm not sure what we're doing here" or such). You are encouraged to share half- or quarter-ideas at any point -- a lot of time none of us will know exactly where we're going. It's as if we are walking in some beautiful hilly open area -- there are some trails, and some of us have walked some of the trails more than others, but none of us have been to all points on all of the hills. We will have planned where the discussion may end up and we'll be striking a balance between letting people "wander" and ensuring that we end up seeing some nice views. IMPORTANT DETAIL! If you want to be able to effectively interact with our shared whiteboard you probably want to use a tablet. We recommend the $69 Wacom Bamboo Pen (http:// www.wacom.com/en/ Products/ BambooTablets/ BambooPen.aspx) and have a few to share. We will happily meet and give them to interested people in San Francisco, just let us know. ALSO! If you want to participate, you should probably have a reasonable understanding of basic arithmetic including perhaps some understanding of why long multiplication work. You should _not_ know what it means to say that -2 is congruent to 4 (mod 3). [Though anyone can observe!]
