Wiser Together: Collaborating Across Generations
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
These are times that are calling the generations to come together. Join Generation Waking Up, Thrive Bay Area, Impact Hub Oakland, and community partners for a special event called "Wiser Together - Collaborating Across Generations." This will be the first event in a series of intergenerational convenings bringing together youth, adults, and elders from around Oakland and the Bay Area. The purpose of this series is to build community connections across generations, to unleash innovative solutions, and to support intergenerational collaboration around local community projects. During this first event, we will use the World Café conversation methodology to explore the questions: What are the gifts we each bring as a generation? What are effective approaches for intergenerational collaboration? What is our vision for the future of our communities and how can we work together to make it happen? Live music and performing artists will kick the night off. Snacks and beverages will also be provided. Tickets are a sliding scale of $10-20, and no one will be turned away for lack of funds. People of all ages ready to engage in community conversation are welcome. Bring a younger. Bring an elder. Bring your community! Please send any questions and inquires to hanni@generationwaki ngup.org. Co-Sponsors: Generation Waking Up is a nonprofit organization igniting young changemakers to bring forth a thriving, just, and sustainable world. Thrive Bay Area is a local initiative unleashing creativity and collaboration for more just, beautiful, and resilient communities. Impact Hub Oakland is an inspiring working space, entrepreneurial incubator, and membership-based community of social engaged people pioneering solutions for a sustainable and equitable world. The World Cafe Community Foundation (www.worldcafe.org) is nonprofit organization transforming the world for the benefit of all life through convening and supporting collaborative conversations globally.
