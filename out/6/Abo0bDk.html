Technology and Your Brain
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
Living in a 24/7 technologically wired society is literally re-wiring our brains. Do you have some type of glowing screen in front of you for all or most of the day? Do you work in front of a computer, check your smartphone when you’re at lunch, and then turn a computer or TV screen (or both) on at night? Are you aware of a sense of imbalance between your mind and body? Do you feel anxious, restless or easily distracted? Are you finding that you are developing digestive, physical, or emotional problems? Do you want to improve both your mental and physical performance? In this class we will discuss the influence of technology on the neurological development and function of the brain, and how to create balance for long-term health and wellness. Rachman Chung< DC, DACNB, FACFN Board Certified Chiropractic Neurologist Fellow of the American College of Functional Neurology Rachman Chung is a chiropractic neurologist and natural medicine practitioner. He specializes in sport and spine injuries and complex neurological conditions ranging from balance and movement problems, learning and behavior disorders and neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's, Parkinson's and dementia. As a chiropractic neurologist he looks at the impact the environment has upon human development in health and disease, and utilizes the latest advances in laboratory techniques to measure early bio-markers of disease. *This class is the first meeting of a 3 week series. Attendance is free, come as you are!

FIlm Fool Gathering 8 - "The Advice of Strangers"
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
FilmFool Gathering 8 is happy to invite you to a screening of "The Advice of Strangers" by Dillon Petrillo amongst a close circle of friends and independent cinema supporters. Dillon has been a frequent featured guest at our screenings and we are happy to see his second feature come to light. The program includes a slide installation by Alex Greenburg about the making of "The Advice of Strangers", a short film "Deer and the Antelope" by Dillon Petrillo that led him to receive a scholarship to actually make his feature film, and finally, the screening of the "Advice of Strangers". Q&A with Dillon after the screening. Based on Franz Kafka's Description of a Struggle, the film is divided into three chapters. The first chapter follows Fran, a bored twentysomething, and George, a starry-eyed lover, on a drunken search for a girl after a New Years Eve party. In the second chapter, Fran meets a hopeful drifter, Rio, who tells her the story of The Supplicant and how he inspired Rio to migrate. The third chapter returns to Fran and George's drunken quest. Shot on location in Cheyenne, WY, Laramie, WY, and San Francisco, CA, The Advice of Strangers is a biting and ethereal look at hope, frustration, wanderlust, assumptions, and love. http://vimeo.com/ dillonpetrillo/ theadviceofstrangerst railer $3-$10 sliding scale Doors open at 7PM Libations provided.
