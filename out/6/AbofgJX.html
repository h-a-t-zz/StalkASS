Khalik Allah: Street Opera @ Heron Arts
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
Please join us for the opening reception of Street Opera, an exhibition of still photography from Khalik Allah, at Heron Arts from 6-9pm! Khalik Allah's Street Opera is a vivid and visceral portrayal of urban life and the human characters that inhabit it. Shooting at 125th and Lexington in Harlem, Allah's street photography gives a powerful glimpse into the spirit and character of those living in poverty. The evening will also feature performance by live jazz quartet Acme Anvil Drop. Heron Arts is pleased to present this work in association with Yerba Buena Center for the Arts, as an opening reception for their afternoon screening of Allah's film work Field Niggas. View Field Niggas at Yerba Buena Center for the Arts Saturday August 15th, 4-6pm. Notable press: The New York Times - http://nyti.ms/ 1Eqw8nt TIME - http://ti.me/1MYD3au

Groupmuse at Monument: Blithe Ignorance of the Future Cataclysm
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
Come lie on carpets and pillows and enjoy an evening of curious and beautiful string quartets. This is the first Groupmuse of Monument, a large and creative community space in San Francisco. Bring your loved ones, and please RSVP at https:// www.groupmuse.com/ events/3318 so we can calculate the correct carpet radius :-) This event is 21+ only. Program: Terry Riley, "Sunrise of the Planetary Dream Collector" Shostakovich Quartet No. 3 Beethoven Quartet No. 1 7:30pm doors 8pm music begins! Groupmuse asks for a $10 suggested donation for the musicians. Some stories: ......... #1 It's 1946. Dmitriy Shostakovich has just had his Symphony no. 9 censured by Soviet authorities. He has written a new string quartet, the haunting and challenging No. 3. For its premiere, most likely so that he would not be accused of "formalism" or "elitism," Shostakovich renames the movements in the manner of a war story: Blithe ignorance of the future cataclysm Rumblings of unrest and anticipation Forces of war unleashed In memory of the dead The eternal question: Why? And for what? ......... #2 It's 1980. In twenty years, NASA will commission you to take sounds of space and weave them into music. This sounds will come from plasma wave receivers flown on a variety of Milky Way spacecraft over a period of 40 years. That is yet to come: for now, you are writing Sunrise of the Planetary Dream Collector. It is an improvisational piece, inspired by a whimsical idea: each day, a dream collector collects the dreams of all the planet's sleepers, and redistributes them again next day. You write the composition for the Kronos Quartet, your new collaborators. It is in Dorian mode, in fourteen-beat modules (because seven!). They players choose the modules they are inspired to play.
