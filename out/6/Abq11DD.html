C0un7Ry s1n9ER: A Taylor Swift ASCII Art Show
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
Thank you all for coming to the opening! It was wonderful talking with all of you about art, life, music, and Taylor Swift. **If you took any pictures or videos, please post them to the event! I would love to see them.** The show will be up for a bit longer, so you're welcome to come for a private viewing. Any inquiries should be directed to Patti Maciesz patti@thegenerator.c o ------------------ Artist and developer Dmitri Cherniak presents a new series of work titled, “C0un7Ry s1n9ER”. Using code, Cherniak manipulates the ASCII text system (letters, symbols, and numbers) to generate a sombre and unlikely palette for his muse. From a distance, the giant images seem pixelated, but they are made up of ASCII characters which as you move closer to the work, abstract the image itself. We are presented with an icon, Taylor Swift, but she is seen through a darker lense. In the tradition of Andy Warhol’s Marilyn, or perhaps even more so Chuck Close, these computer generated portraits can only exist in the present day. They are a lucid look at what a pop icon looks like now and how she is interpreted. Hashed and rehashed online, memed, decoded, their personal photos hacked. Taylor is a starlet in a very different time than Marilyn.
