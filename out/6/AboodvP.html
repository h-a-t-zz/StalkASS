PANEL MODERATOR Neil Anderson is a Technology Strategist, Entrepreneur and early-stage investor, with in-depth experience in Enterprise business models and impact-driven technologies, has worked in over 20 countries and has detailed knowledge of the startups hubs in San Francisco, New York and London. A Founding investor of Angel Round Capital Investment L.P., the first Angel-led venture fund in New York, Neil has also been variously an Advisor and Mentor to innovation programs for NY entrepreneurs (Entrepreneur Roundtable Accelerator), for women investors (Pipeline Fellowship), for civic innovation (Personal Democracy Forum) and for Healthcare & Hardware startup founders (Startup Leadership Program.) Neil has MA and BA degrees in Classics from Cambridge University. BEST PRACTICES PANELISTS Jenny Kassan, CEO of Cutting Edge Capital. Jenny has over nineteen years of experience as an attorney for and creator of social enterprises. She is the CEO of Cutting Edge Capital, a consulting firm that helps social ventures raise capital in alignment with their goals and values. Jenny is also an attorney at Cutting Edge Counsel, a law firm that serves social enterprise. Her legal practice areas include small business start-up and financing, securities regulation, nonprofit law, and cooperatives. Jenny earned a masters degree in City and Regional Planning from the University of California at Berkeley and earned her J.D. from Yale Law School. Danny Spitzberg, Principal at Peak Agency, a crowdfunding collective running campaigns with grassroots causes and ventures in the Bay Area. He has 6 years of experience in impact model development, community and online organizing, and campaign strategy. Danny is also a core organizer with Omni Commons (www.omnicommons.org ), a collective of collectives stewarding a beautiful community center in Oakland's Temescal neighborhood. Barbara Jefferson, Brave Space - Barbara is an experience designer, facilitator, and trainer; specializing in The Work That Reconnects, leadership development, Theater of the Oppressed, and youth empowerment. As a queer person of color, B is committed to using these gifts to co-power all people, with an emphasis on queer, youth, and POC communities. Barbara has designed experiential leadership curriculum and facilitates transformative learning experiences for emerging change makers committed to social justice, sustainability, and human thriving. Barbara is passionate about creating “brave spaces” where individuals can explore their authenticity, strengthen their courage, and take steps toward holistic and community centered leadership. PROJECT SHOWCASE Generation Waking Up. Building on four years of successful youth workshops and leadership trainings, Generation Waking Up is rallying funding and support to create ReGeneration, a high school curriculum that will empower teachers to take the impact of this work directly into their classrooms. Through interactive and adaptable learning modules, students will gain an understanding of today’s global challenges and opportunities, develop 21st century leadership skills, identify their role in creating positive change, and access the tools and resources needed to make a difference at their school and in their community. Pacha's Pajamas. The award-winning story, Pacha's Pajamas, is nutritious children's entertainment that builds self-esteem, affinity to nature and encourages kids to create the world they want to live in. Pacha's magical pajamas whisk her off to imaginary worlds where she journeys with the characters on her pajamas. We are raising funds to produce the world's first interactive pajamas and their companion app and story book. Positioning the app over the pajamas and book brings the characters and their stories to life. Terra Nova. We design, build and maintain ecological landscapes and gardens in harmony with nature - the Tread Lightly service. This is fossil free landscaping at its finest. Bay Bucks. We enable businesses to purchase from other businesses that have excess inventory or extra capacity at a discount. We envision a greater Bay Area where sustainable regional economic growth and development are in service of the people and the environment, rather than corporate profits. We envision an economy that promotes stewardship of our land and local resources, supports local business and innovation, drives re-localization of production, and recreates the market from being merely transactional to a place for community building.
