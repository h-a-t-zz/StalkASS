Journey to the End of the Night: San Francisco 2013
Dustin Moskovitz was invited
IMPORTANT: This game will have a maximum attendance of 3,000. Online pre-registration is required, but it does not guarantee entry. Entry will be first-come, first-served, and anyone without a printed and signed waiver will not be able to participate. (If you have not yet registered online and received your waiver, use the link below.) We will start handing out materials by 6pm at Everett Middle School, and the game begins at 7pm, so arrive early to guarantee your participation! ONLINE PRE-REGISTRATION IS REQUIRED THIS YEAR AT: http:// sfjourney.herokuapp.c om/ The seventh edition of Journey to the End of the Night returns to the shadowy cityscape of San Francisco as part of the Come Out and Play festival. Last year, 2,000 participants helped take over city streets in the name of play, and this year players will once more dash down hidden alleys, encounter mysterious strangers, and change how they experience the city. Journey to the End of the Night is a free street game played across cities around the world, spreading from San Francisco to Chicago, Washington, DC, Milan, Berlin, Mexico City, Christchurch, and beyond. Players sporting colored ribbons will race through a series of nonlinear checkpoints scattered throughout the city, while avoiding being caught by chasers. Those who survive will be praised and feted at the end party. Those who fall will become chasers themselves, rising in undeath to pursue the remaining runners. This year, there will be two colors of runners, each competing to get as many of their color as possible to the end point. For one night, drop your relations, your work and leisure activities, and all your usual motives for movement and action, and let yourself be drawn by the attractions of the chase and the encounters you find there. No bicycles, no skates, no cars, no taxis. You may only travel on foot or by public transportation. Wear comfortable shoes, and bring water. Prizes will be awarded for top finishers and best chasers. About the Come Out and Play Festival The annual Come Out and Play Festival seeks to provide a forum for new types of public games and play. Its mission is to gather designers of innovative new games and experiences, and bring those experiences to a public eager to rediscover the world around them. Started in New York City in 2006, 2013 is its fourth year in San Francisco. Journey to the End of the Night is part of Come Out and Play.
